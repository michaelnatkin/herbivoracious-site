<p><a href="/images/old/6a00e008d618bb8834014e8880c712970d-800wi.webp" rel="lightbox" style="display: inline;"><img alt="Three Easy Banchan" class="asset  asset-image at-xid-6a00e008d618bb8834014e8880c712970d" src="/images/old/6a00e008d618bb8834014e8880c712970d-500wi.webp" title="Three Easy Banchan" /></a><br /><em>Three Easy Banchan (Korean Side Dishes)</em></p> <p>No Korean meal is complete without a selection of at least a few <em>banchan</em>&#0160;- the small side dishes that are served in the middle of the table for everyone to enjoy. Banchan are usually chilled or at room temperature</p> <p>There are hundreds of different banchan recipes, many of which can be whipped up in just a few minutes. Once you get an idea of the flavor palette, you&#39;ll be able to improvise your own. Here are three of my current favorites (all vegan).</p> <p>Here are a few Korean or Korean-inspired dishes that these banchan would go well with: <a href="/2010/06/mandu-koreanstyle-potstickers-with-ssamjang-kochujang-sauce-recipe/" target="_blank">mandu (Korean potstickers)</a>, <a href="/2010/06/tofu-and-kimchi-dinner-for-one-recipe/" target="_blank">tofu &amp; kimchi bowl</a>, <a href="/2010/04/vegetarian-japchae-a-guest-recipe-by-alice-of-savory-sweet-life/" target="_blank">vegetarian japchae</a>, and the kimchi stew that will be in <a href="/2010/10/im-writing-a-cookbook-and-you-can-win-prizes/" target="_blank">my upcoming book</a>!</p> <p><strong>Bean sprouts with sesame oil</strong></p> <ul> <li>2 cups bean sprouts</li> <li>1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil</li> <li>Sea salt</li> </ul> <p>Bring a small pot of salted water to a boil and blanch the bean sprouts for 90 seconds. Drain, rinse in cool water, drain again and pat dry, then toss with toasted sesame oil and a big pinch of sea salt. Chill.&#0160;</p> <p><strong>Squash in sweet soy sauce</strong></p> <ul> <li>1 small delicata squash, halved, seeded, cut into 1/4&quot; thick half moons</li> <li>1/3 cup soy sauce</li> <li>1/4 cup sugar</li> <li>2 tablespoons rice vinegar</li> <li>1-inch piece of ginger, grated</li> <li>1 thinly sliced jalapeno pepper (optional)</li> </ul> <p>In a small saucepan, cover squash with water, bring to a boil, and simmer until nearly tender, about 5 minutes. Drain. Mix soy sauce, sugar, rice vinegar and ginger. Pour over the squash and bring back to a simmer; cook until fully tender. Chill. Stir in jalapeno pepper. Serve with only a small amount of the liquid. You can also do this with sweet potato.</p> <div class="recipe-card">
<p><strong>Spicy stir-fried zucchini</strong></p> <ul> <li>1 tablespoon toasted sesame oil</li> <li>1 small zucchini, 1/2-inch dice</li> <li>1/4 teaspoon Kosher salt</li> <li>1 teaspoon <a href="https://www.amazon.com/dp/B07BDRMFDZ?tag=herb-hugo-20">gochujang</a></li> <li>1/2-inch piece ginger, grated</li> <li>1 teaspoon toasted black sesame seeds</li> </ul> <p><strong>&#0160;</strong>Heat a small skillet over high heat. Add toasted sesame oil. When it shimmers, add the zucchini, salt, gochujang and ginger. Stir fry until crisp-tender, about 1 minute. Transfer to a bowl and mix in sesame seeds. Chill.</p> <p>&#0160;</p>
