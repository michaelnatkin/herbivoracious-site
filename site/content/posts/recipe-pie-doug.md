<p><a href="/images/2007/12/06/piedoughandganachecookietower.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img width="455" height="302" border="0" alt="Piedoughandganachecookietower" title="Piedoughandganachecookietower" src="/images/2007-small/12/06/piedoughandganachecookietower.jpg" /></a></p>  <p>Let's consider the problem:</p>  <ol><li>I have a bit of leftover flaky pie crust dough</li>  <li>I really want something chocolatey, but quick</li>  <li>I'm obsessed with circle cutters</li></ol>  <p>Solution:</p>  <p>The cookies above. Recipe: Cut out pie dough with a successive series of cutters, starting at maybe 2.5 inches down to the smallest size you have. Bake til golden brown. Heat up a small amount of cream, pour it over finely chopped chocolate, wait a minute, whisk, spread on the bottom of all of the circles except the largest, stack 'em up, watch Get Smart reruns while unstacking and experiencing mild glee.</p>  <p>Extra credit:</p>  <p>Use a squeeze bottle to decorate a plate with any extra ganache.</p>  <p><a href="/images/2007/12/06/piecrustandganachecookie2.jpg" rel="lightbox"><img width="455" height="302" border="0" alt="Piecrustandganachecookie2" title="Piecrustandganachecookie2" src="/images/2007-small/12/06/piecrustandganachecookie2.jpg" /></a>   </p>
